Asking Questions at Training How to ask about anything in Creo

How do I...? (Jak...?)

"How do I create a pin joint?"
Jak vytvořím čepový spoj?
"How do I add a servo motor?"
Jak přidám servomotor?
"How do I run the analysis?"
Jak spustím analýzu?
"How do I change the time step?"
Jak změním časový krok?
"How do I export the animation?"
Jak exportuji animaci?

Where do I find...? (Kde najdu...?)

"Where do I find the mechanism tab?"
Kde najdu záložku mechanismu?
"Where is the gravity setting?"
Kde je nastavení gravitace?
"Where can I see the results?"
Kde můžu vidět výsledky?

What is / What does...? (Co je...?)

"What is a degree of freedom?"
Co je stupeň volnosti?
"What does this error message mean?"
Co znamená tato chybová hláška?
"What is the difference between kinematic and dynamic analysis?"
Jaký je rozdíl mezi kinematickou a dynamickou analýzou?

Problem Reporting (Hlášení problémů)

"I get an error when I try to run the analysis."
Mám chybu, když zkouším spustit analýzu.
"My assembly has too many degrees of freedom."
Moje sestava má příliš mnoho stupňů volnosti.
"The servo motor doesn't move the part."
Servomotor nepohybuje součástí.
"The mechanism is locked / overconstrained."
Mechanismus je zamčený / převázaný.
"It worked before but now it doesn't regenerate."
Předtím to fungovalo, ale teď to neregeneruje.

Understanding Instructor's Directions What the instructor will say and what it means

Tip: Instructor Commands Pattern

Instructors typically say: "Go to [Menu] > [Submenu] > [Option]" or "Click on [Button]" or "Select [Object] in the model tree". Listen for the menu/button name — even if you miss other words, you can find it in the UI.

Common Instructor Phrases

"Go to Applications and switch to Mechanism."
Jděte do Applications a přepněte na Mechanism.
"Select the axis of the hole."
Vyberte osu díry.
"Now define the connection type as Pin."
Teď definujte typ spoje jako Pin.
"Set the velocity to 360 degrees per second."
Nastavte rychlost na 360 stupňů za sekundu.
"Hit the play button to see the animation."
Stiskněte tlačítko play pro zobrazení animace.
"Make sure there are zero degrees of freedom."
Ujistěte se, že je nula stupňů volnosti.
"You need to assemble the part before adding motion."
Musíte sestavu sestavit před přidáním pohybu.

Asking for Clarification

"Sorry, which button do I click?"
Promiňte, na které tlačítko mám kliknout?
"Do you mean this one?" (pointing at screen)
Myslíte toto? (ukazuje na obrazovku)
"Should I select the surface or the edge?"
Mám vybrat plochu nebo hranu?
"I can't find that option in my menu."
Nemůžu najít tu možnost v mém menu.
"Wait, I'm one step behind."
Počkejte, jsem o krok pozadu.
"Can we take a short break?"
Můžeme si dát krátkou pauzu?

Practice Dialogues Realistic training scenarios to rehearse

Scenario 1: Asking about a connection

Instructor:"Now we'll define the connection between the crank and the base. Select the axis of the pin."
You:"Sorry, which axis? The one on the crank or the base?"
(Promiňte, která osa? Ta na klice nebo na základně?)
Instructor:"The one on the crank first, then the base. They need to be aligned."
You:"OK, I selected both. What connection type should I use?"
Instructor:"Use a Pin joint — it allows rotation around one axis."

Scenario 2: Reporting an error

You:"Excuse me, I have a problem. When I run the analysis, I get an error."
(Promiňte, mám problém. Když spustím analýzu, zobrazí se mi chyba.)
Instructor:"What does the error message say?"
You:"It says 'redundant constraints detected'."
Instructor:"That means your mechanism is overconstrained. Check your connection definitions — you probably have too many constraints on one joint."
You:"How do I fix it? Should I remove one constraint?"

Scenario 3: Coffee break small talk

Instructor:"So, where are you from?"
You:"I'm from the Czech Republic, from Prague."
Instructor:"Nice! How long have you been using Creo?"
You:"About five years. But this is my first time with Motion."
(Asi pět let. Ale s Motion pracuji poprvé.)
Instructor:"Don't worry, we'll start from the basics."

Scenario 4: Lunch / Dinner

Instructor:"We have a one-hour lunch break. There's a restaurant downstairs."
You:"Thank you. Is there a menu in English?"
(Děkuji. Je tam menu v angličtině?)
Instructor:"Yes, they have an English menu. The lunch special is usually good."
You:"Great. What time do we start again?"
Instructor:"We resume at 1:30."

30-Day Preparation Plan What to practice each week

Week 1: Survival Basics

  • Learn all 10 Survival Phrases by heart
  • Practice saying: "Could you repeat that?" naturally
  • Learn greetings and introductions
  • Do 2x AriaEnglishTutor sessions (Creo Training scenario)
  • Learn numbers 1-100 in English (for dimensions)

Week 2: Creo Vocabulary

  • Learn all Joint types (pin, slider, cylinder...)
  • Learn Mechanism components (servo, spring, damper...)
  • Learn Analysis terms (kinematic, dynamic, DOF...)
  • Flashcard drill: 15 min daily
  • Do 3x AriaEnglishTutor sessions

Week 3: Questions & Problems

  • Practice "How do I...?" sentences (5 per day)
  • Practice reporting problems ("I get an error...")
  • Rehearse all 4 Practice Dialogues out loud
  • Learn Creo UI vocabulary (menus, buttons)
  • Do 4x AriaEnglishTutor sessions

Week 4: Full Simulation

  • Simulate a full training day with Aria (30 min session)
  • Practice small talk (coffee, lunch, hotel)
  • Review all flashcards — aim for 90%+ correct
  • Practice understanding instructor directions
  • Final review of Survival Phrases
  • Pack confidence — you're ready!

Pro Tip: Don't aim for perfection

At A1 level, your goal is communication, not grammar. If people understand you, you're succeeding. The instructor has taught many non-native speakers and will be patient. The most important skill is asking when you don't understand — practice that the most.
